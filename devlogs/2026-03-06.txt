Title: Creatures actually move now
TL;DR: Fixed completely broken creature pathfinding — specimens now roam the entire facility through doorways and detect the player properly.
---
Bug fixes:
- Escaped specimens were stuck in their room forever — the navigation graph had zero connections between rooms and hallways, so pathfinding always failed silently
- Hallway ring was broken at corners (overlapping waypoints weren't connected)
- Specimen detection range wasn't scaling up over time as intended
- Specimens now path through doorways like the player does — room center → door → hallway → other rooms

Gameplay changes:
- Specimens pause less between waypoints (0.5-2s instead of 2-4s) so they feel more restless and cover the facility faster

New features:
- Dev panel (press + key) — skip straight to night with a specimen in containment, or trigger an escape instantly

Chase AI overhaul:
- Creatures now charge straight at you when close instead of zig-zagging through waypoints — feels way more aggressive
- No more stuttering at hallway corners — creatures glide through turns smoothly
- Much harder to shake off: creatures pursue for longer (12s vs 8s) and track your movement better
- When a creature loses sight of you, it goes to where it last SAW you — not where you actually are. Hiding around a corner actually works now

No more idle creatures during alert/chase:
- Specimens now walk toward you during the yellow alert phase instead of standing still staring
- If you duck out of sight during yellow, the specimen investigates where it saw you instead of going back to idle
- After hitting you, the specimen only pauses for 1 second (was 2) then immediately resumes the chase
- Investigating specimens now patrol the area instead of standing in one spot for 8 seconds — they check nearby rooms and hallways

Creature speed + door awareness:
- Chase speed rebalanced — specimens are now between walking and sprinting speed, so you can outrun them but it's tense
- Specimens now open doors when they walk through them — you'll hear the door slide open as a warning
- Closed doors block specimen vision — hiding behind a closed door actually works now. Opening a door still makes noise though, so they might investigate
- Specimens explore the entire facility instead of looping the same hallway — they pick distant destinations and prioritise hallways

Yellow alert feels snappier:
- Alert-to-chase transition is faster (0.5s instead of 1s)
- Investigation patrols are shorter — 2 nearby nodes instead of 4, closer radius
- Less time spent spinning in yellow before either chasing or returning to wander

Death screen:
- Removed the jumpscare sequence (flash, ASCII close-up, TV static) — death now fades straight to black with a retry screen
- Retry returns to the main menu instead of restarting the night directly

Main menu:
- Background now shows the elevator shaft with scrolling bands — same descent illusion as the night intro

Lure system:
- Lure pickup in storage room is now a visible ASCII billboard sprite instead of an invisible trigger
- Lure shows as a task marker on the minimap — first shows pickup location, then shows the deposit location in the containment room
- Carrying the lure shows a green pathfinding line on the minimap guiding you to the target containment room

UI polish:
- Containment room care buttons (Feed/Water/Play) moved from the glass panel to the wall on the right side of the glass, with green signs labeling each button
- Cart quest now only highlights the cart itself instead of the entire elevator room floor
- Specimen care items (drag-and-drop) moved to the top-right corner of the habitat view with a "DRAG TO HABITAT" hint

Charging dock improvements:
- Charging station now covers the center desk in the command center — much easier to find and interact with
- Desk glows green (edges + emissive tint) when battery drops below 20%, with distance-based fade
- Minimap shows escort path to the charging desk at 20% battery instead of waiting until 0%

Quality of life:
- Minimap escort paths are now yellow/amber instead of green — easier to distinguish from the player triangle and room walls
- Escort path lines now follow the actual hallway layout — clean right-angle turns through doorways and corridors instead of wobbly waypoint-to-waypoint paths
- Main menu elevator view now matches the night intro height — no more jarring visual jump when starting a night
- Doors auto-open when you walk near them (~2.5 units) — no more mashing E at every doorway. They stay open while you're nearby and auto-close after you leave
- Device no longer traps your cursor — mouse moves freely across the whole screen when the device is open. Click anywhere outside the device to close it
- WASD no longer moves the player while the device is open (was especially noticeable when playing Snake)
- Egg display on the device no longer has a stretched look or ugly green grid — eggs now sit cleanly centered on a dark background
- Device now opens by looking down — tilt your view all the way down and the device pops up naturally, like glancing at a wrist screen. Move your cursor above the device to put it away. Tab still works as a shortcut
- Camera can't look as far down anymore (capped at ~60°) — looking all the way down is now the device trigger
- Specimens no longer give up and wander away after hitting you — they recover faster from the attack pause (0.4s vs 1s), knockback is less extreme, and they track you omnidirectionally for 2 seconds after resuming the chase

Night progression:
- Food, water, and toy placements now carry over between nights — if Nibbles had food in night 1, it's still there in night 2
- If a specimen is missing an item, a fetch task spawns for it. If multiple specimens need the same item (e.g. both need food), you can deposit it in either room — the escort path guides you to the closer one
- Broken camera repairs now show as task markers on the minimap, and the camera prop glows green so you can spot it in the room
- No more clock-out terminal — when your shift ends, the elevator opens and you just walk into it to end the night. Much more natural than finding and pressing E on a terminal
